# Circle of Competence

Origin: Warren Buffett & Charlie Munger. "Know your circle of competence, and stick within it. The size of that circle is not very important; knowing its boundaries, however, is vital."

## When to Use

- Deciding whether to take on a new challenge or opportunity
- Evaluating if you're qualified to make a judgment on something
- Career decisions — should you specialize deeper or broaden?
- Feeling imposter syndrome (might be a real signal, not just anxiety)

## The Process

### Step 1: Map your circle
What do you genuinely know deeply? Not "have read about" — deeply, from experience.
- What could you teach without preparation?
- Where do people come to you for advice?
- What mistakes have you learned from firsthand?

### Step 2: Map the edges
Where does your competence start getting thin?
- Where do you rely on secondhand knowledge?
- Where would you need to look things up?
- Where have you been wrong and didn't see it coming?

### Step 3: Map the outside
What are you clearly NOT competent in but may be pretending to be?
- Dunning-Kruger zones — areas where you know just enough to be dangerous
- Areas where you have opinions but no experience

### Step 4: Decide your strategy
| Position | Strategy |
|----------|----------|
| Deep inside circle | Leverage — this is your moat |
| At the edge | Learn deliberately or find an expert |
| Outside circle | Either invest to learn or stay out |

## The Boundary Is the Value

The power isn't in having a large circle. It's in knowing exactly where the edge is.

- Inside the circle: confident decisions, high signal
- At the edge: conscious incompetence — you know what you don't know
- Outside the circle: danger zone — you don't know what you don't know

## Example Application

**Situation:** "Should I lead the ML infrastructure redesign?"
- Inside circle: distributed systems, data pipelines, system design
- Edge: ML training loops, model serving optimization
- Outside: ML research, model architecture choices

**Insight:** Lead the infra parts, partner with ML experts on the model-specific parts. Don't pretend competence at the model layer.

## Pitfalls

- **Circle too small**: Imposter syndrome shrinks your perceived circle. Reality-check with feedback.
- **Circle too large**: Overconfidence expands it. Look for where you've been surprised.
- **Static thinking**: Circles grow with deliberate practice. Don't treat boundaries as permanent.

## Cross-validates with

- **Johari Window** — Circle of Competence maps knowledge; Johari maps self-awareness
- **Growth Mindset** — Circles can expand, but knowing current boundaries is prerequisite
