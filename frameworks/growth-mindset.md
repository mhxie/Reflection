# Growth Mindset

Origin: Carol Dweck (Stanford). The belief that abilities can be developed through dedication and hard work — versus a fixed mindset that sees abilities as innate and unchangeable.

## When to Use

- Facing a challenge that feels beyond your ability
- Processing failure or negative feedback
- Comparing yourself to others unfavorably
- Deciding whether to attempt something new and difficult
- Noticing avoidance of challenges

## Fixed vs Growth Mindset Signals

| Fixed Mindset Signal | Growth Mindset Reframe |
|---------------------|----------------------|
| "I'm not good at X" | "I'm not good at X yet" |
| "This is too hard" | "This requires more effort and strategy" |
| "I failed" | "I learned what doesn't work" |
| "They're just naturally talented" | "They've practiced more than I can see" |
| "Feedback is criticism" | "Feedback is data" |
| "If I have to try hard, I must not be smart enough" | "Effort is how mastery develops" |
| "I'll stick to what I know" | "I'll expand what I know" |

## The Process

### Step 1: Catch the fixed mindset voice
Notice when you're thinking in fixed terms:
- "I can't do this"
- "This isn't my strength"
- "I should already be better at this"

### Step 2: Reframe with growth language
- Replace "can't" with "haven't learned to yet"
- Replace judgment with curiosity: "What would help me improve?"
- Replace comparison with trajectory: "Where was I 6 months ago?"

### Step 3: Design the learning strategy
Growth mindset without strategy is just optimism. Pair with:
- Deliberate practice (focused effort on weak points)
- Feedback loops (how will you know you're improving?)
- Milestones (break "get good at X" into measurable steps)

### Step 4: Normalize struggle
The discomfort of learning IS the process, not a sign of inadequacy.
- "This is hard because it's new, not because I'm incapable"
- The struggle zone is where growth happens

## Effort vs Strategy

Dweck's update: pure effort isn't enough. The right strategy matters.

| Approach | Result |
|----------|--------|
| Effort without strategy | Burnout, frustration |
| Strategy without effort | Knowledge without skill |
| Effort + strategy | Growth |
| Effort + strategy + feedback | Accelerated growth |

## Application to Goal Review

When reviewing goals, check for fixed-mindset framing:
- Is a goal abandoned because "I'm not that kind of person"? (Fixed)
- Is effort being directed without feedback? (Effort trap)
- Are you avoiding goals in new domains? (Comfort zone protection)

## Pitfalls

- **Toxic positivity**: "Just believe and you'll succeed" isn't growth mindset. Real growth acknowledges difficulty.
- **Effort worship**: Working hard at the wrong thing isn't growth. Strategy matters.
- **Dismissing talent**: Talent exists. Growth mindset means talent is a starting point, not a ceiling.
- **Weaponizing it**: "You just need more growth mindset" is dismissive of real structural barriers.

## Cross-validates with

- **Circle of Competence** — Growth mindset says circles can expand; Circle of Competence says know where you currently stand
- **Double-Loop Learning** — Growth mindset questions "can I learn this?" Double-loop questions "should I learn this?"
