# Inversion (Munger's Mental Model)

Origin: Charlie Munger / Carl Jacobi. "Invert, always invert." Instead of asking how to succeed, ask how to fail — then avoid that.

## When to Use

- Planning a major initiative and want to stress-test it
- Trying to solve a problem and direct approaches aren't working
- Evaluating a decision where the downside matters more than the upside
- Need to identify blind spots in your thinking

## The Process

### Step 1: State the goal clearly
"I want to [achieve X]"

### Step 2: Invert the question
"How would I guarantee failure at X?" or "What would make X impossible?"

### Step 3: List failure paths
Be specific and creative. Think about:
- What behaviors would guarantee failure?
- What assumptions, if wrong, would sink the plan?
- What has caused similar efforts to fail historically?

### Step 4: Check your current plan against the failure list
Are you doing any of the failure-guaranteed behaviors? Are you relying on fragile assumptions?

### Step 5: Design around failure
Modify your plan to avoid the top failure paths.

## Example Application

**Goal:** "Succeed in my first 90 days at a new job"
**Inverted:** "How would I guarantee failure in my first 90 days?"
- Never ask questions, pretend I know everything
- Ignore team norms and impose my old company's way
- Take on too much, deliver nothing
- Skip 1:1s with my manager
- Don't learn the codebase before proposing changes

**Insight:** Several of these are tempting failure modes for experienced hires.

## Power of Inversion

- Humans are better at identifying failure than predicting success
- Avoidance of stupidity is easier than pursuit of brilliance
- Failure modes are often shared across domains; success patterns are specific

## Pitfalls

- **Excessive caution**: Inversion highlights risks but shouldn't paralyze action
- **Missing upside**: Avoiding failure ≠ achieving greatness. Pair with a positive framework.

## Cross-validates with

- **Pre-Mortem** — Pre-mortem imagines failure happened; Inversion engineers failure paths
- **First Principles** — Inversion clears bad ideas; First Principles builds good ones
