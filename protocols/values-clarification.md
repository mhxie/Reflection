# Values Clarification Protocol

Helps the user surface and prioritize their actual values — not stated values, but revealed values (what their behavior shows they care about).

## When to Use

- During annual or life-transition reviews
- When goals feel hollow or conflicting
- When the user is making a major life decision
- When there's a persistent gap between intention and action

## The Process

### Step 1: Surface Stated Values
From the user's notes, identify what they say they value:
- Goal statements ("I want to...")
- Principles they reference ("I believe...")
- Repeated themes across notes

### Step 2: Surface Revealed Values
From the user's behavior (as reflected in daily notes and activity):
- Where they actually spend time
- What they consistently choose when forced to prioritize
- What they protect when things get busy
- What they sacrifice when under pressure

### Step 3: Compare Stated vs. Revealed

| Value | Stated Importance | Revealed Importance | Gap |
|-------|------------------|--------------------|----|
| [Value] | High/Med/Low | High/Med/Low | [alignment/gap] |

**Gaps are the insight.** Where stated and revealed values diverge, there's either:
- A genuine conflict (values compete for finite time)
- Self-deception (stated value is aspirational, not real)
- A hidden competing commitment (Immunity to Change)

### Step 4: Rank by Revealed Behavior

Ask the user to rank their values by how they actually behave, not how they wish they behaved:

1. When time is scarce, what do you protect?
2. When you have unexpected free time, what do you do?
3. When you're stressed, what do you cut first?
4. What would you refuse to give up, even for a significant gain elsewhere?

### Step 5: Values-Goal Alignment Check

For each active goal, check:
- Does this goal serve a top-3 value?
- If not, why is it still a goal? (Obligation? Legacy? Others' expectations?)
- Could a different goal serve the same value better?

## Common Value Tensions

| Tension | Both Are Real | The Question |
|---------|--------------|-------------|
| Security vs. Growth | Financial stability vs. career risk | "How much security is enough to take risks?" |
| Achievement vs. Presence | Goals vs. enjoying now | "Are you achieving TO be happy, or INSTEAD of being happy?" |
| Autonomy vs. Belonging | Independence vs. deep relationships | "Where do you need more of each?" |
| Depth vs. Breadth | Mastery vs. exploration | "Which serves your 5-year trajectory?" |
| Individual vs. Collective | Personal goals vs. family/team needs | "Where are you sacrificing, and is it sustainable?" |

## Output Format

```markdown
# Values Clarification — YYYY-MM-DD

## Stated Values (from notes)
1. [Value] — evidence from [[Note]]
2. [Value] — evidence from [[Note]]

## Revealed Values (from behavior)
1. [Value] — evidence: [what the user consistently does]
2. [Value] — evidence: [what the user protects under pressure]

## Alignment Map
| Value | Stated | Revealed | Gap | Notes |
|-------|--------|----------|-----|-------|

## Key Tension
[The most important value tension surfaced]

## Implication for Goals
[Which goals align with top revealed values? Which don't?]
```

## Rules

1. **No judgment on values.** All values are legitimate. The work is alignment, not ranking by morality.
2. **Revealed > stated.** Behavior doesn't lie. But don't be harsh — the gap itself is interesting, not shameful.
3. **Values shift over time.** What mattered at 25 may not matter at 30. That's growth, not inconsistency.
4. **Language sensitivity.** Values discussions can touch identity. Be gentle.
